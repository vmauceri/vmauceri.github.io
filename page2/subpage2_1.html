Travelling Thailand on a Real-Life Budget

Bangkok- City like NYC but with less sky scrapers! It is a very busy city full of life. Amazing food to explore, their wet markets are an experience you do not want to miss as well as their local guided tours of some of the cool sights around the city. Some of the best tours you will experience are the boat tours they offer around the city to really experience the beautiful sights and temples. There is amazing public transportation you can use or overhead walkways if you want to get your steps in. THey also have very nice water fronts to enjoy in the evenings and many museums and attractions to get out of the heat. Their malls are very picturesque as well with budget friendly amazing food courts to experience. Since we are on the topic of foods, a little research goes a long way. Depending where you stay look on google maps for restaurants nearby and filter to your needs. Just to give you an idea, a family of 4 can feast at a local restaurant for under $20 USD. Accommodations can range between $40-$60 for a family of 4. You do not need to rent a car and you will see first hand why when you arrive. They drive very differently than most of the world, especially on small streets so do not even risk it for your and their safety! From Bangkok you can take a short bus ride to a great beach town or hop on a flight for an hour or so and exp[ore their amazing beaches. Some of Thailand's beaches have been movie sets at the box office you would not want to miss. Take the culture in from the local people and stay away from any chain restaurant you may recognize.
