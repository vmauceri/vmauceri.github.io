Travel North America/Canada

There are many places in North America where families can travel without breaking a bank. It doesn’t have to be Disney world all the time. Some of the best places can be around the corner from where you live where you can get local discounts and promotions. Theme Parks are a great example in small towns and off season. Everyone knows Bush Gardens in Florida, but what about Virginia? It is a hidden secret theme park to explore with less wait times than Florida and most likely can fit anyone's budget. If you are looking more up north, Cedar Point Ohio is a good place as well. Has a waterpark and a theme park and sometimes when you get one ticket the other is either free or at a discounted price. It has some of the most extreme rides in the country (especially good for teens and adults) and it also has a theme if you have little ones that can relate to knowing the characters. The best one and most underrated, in my opinion, is Dollywood in Tennessee (TN). It definitely plenty to do, even in the off season, and overall a great place to stay on a budget. It is a big park with lots of shows - unlike any other park- super child friendly with a little southern hospitality. The location of the theme park is in the middle of timeshare capital and you can definitely score tickets or meal vouchers if you are willing to sit through a presentation for an hour and save yourself some money. Worth checking it out for sure!
