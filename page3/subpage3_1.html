Travelling Georgia on a Real-Life Budget

One of the places that is a must go place is the country of Georgia. It is one of the countries where they still have their own currency and the exchange rates are favorable for USD. It is a country full of History, religion, art, mountains and the best food in the world in my opinion. Georgia has great public transportation within the main capital of Tbilisi and even going out to different regions they have trains and buses and vans which are super safe to travel with so that you don’t over spend on tours. It has a seaside if that is what you are looking for as well as a mountain side and metropolitan vibe as well. SOme of the best hotels are with hostels or little boutique ones with exceptional views and hospitality. If traveling with a big family. Air bnb’s are the way to go. Even renting a car is affordable if you choose not to use their very efficient public transportation. Going out to eat in Georgia is not expensive at all. Most meals are served family style and in large portions so that the whole family can enjoy a group of friends traveling together. Most sights are free to enter in Georgia unless they are museums or theaters and sometimes they have special events or dates where the admission may be at a discount or even free. If you go outside the main cities prices get cheaper and cheaper for lodging, eating and the experience is very different. In some villages people may invite you to dinner at no charge at all because hospitality in Georgia is a very big deal. They have some of the best wines in the world and sometimes wine tastings are free. A typical meal in Georgia for a family of 4 can cost you about 100 Lari which is about $38 and comes out pretty full. They have all sorts of meal options, spicy, vegetarian, child friendly, authentic, modern, fusion and so on. Possibilities are endless and the menu option just keeps going. Every region has its main dish or two and even the ones you recognize from other places have its own authentic twist to it. Renting a boutique hotel for a couple can be as cheap as $15USD. Just because it is affordable does not mean it is bad. There are many chain hotels like Marriotts and Radissons but again that comes with the foreign prices. It is always better to go with local stuff to get the full cultural experience and pay a fraction of the price. You can make a trip out of just visiting ORthodox Churches and you will be filled with history and Culture and all churches in Georgia are free to enter. If you do decide to explore the mountains, a tour is almost always recommended due to the road conditions and hidden spots that only locals would know. You can splurge on those a little but even those are very affordable.
