Travelling Saudi Arabia on a Real-Life Budget

You have so many Mosques to explore with a guide that is complementary, so many natural cliffs and desserts to explore. Tour of the Aramco building or any of their sea sides are great to explore. The exchange has not changed in over 30 years or so and therefore spices are pretty affordable there. Since the new King came in power even the dress codes and rules for women have been modernized to an extent. They still do have dress codes for tourists and public behavior but compared to what it used to be a few years back it has improved tremendously. Their local hotels are great to stay and experience their architecture. The food is something that can not be described but tasted. Their shopping malls are pieces of art on their own and again the people are very hospitable. Getting around must be done via your own transportation, so renting a car would be a must but affordable. Their museums are a must to further your knowledge about their culture and of course they have their won type of coffee and tea which must be tasted!
